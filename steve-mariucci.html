All you need to know about Steve Mariucci

Steve Mariucci is an American sportscaster and former football coach, has a net worth of $18 million. Steve Mariucci, sometimes known as “Mooch,” coached at the University of California, Berkeley, as well as the San Francisco 49ers and Detroit Lions in the NFL before becoming an analyst for the NFL Network.

Who is Steve Mariucci?

Steve Mariucci was born on November 4, 1955, and grew up in Iron Mountain, Michigan, where he met his best buddy, future Michigan State University basketball coach Tom Izzo. They attended Iron Mountain High School and competed in football, basketball, and track together. They were even roommates at Northern Michigan University, where Mariucci was a three-time Division II All-American quarterback.

He led the Wildcats to three postseason victories and the national championship as a sophomore in 1975. Steve Mariucci briefly entered the professional ranks in May 1978, when he signed with the Hamilton Tiger-Cats of the Canadian Football League, but he was released the following month.

What is Steve Mariucci’s career?

Steve Mariucci began coaching at Northern Michigan as a running backs coach in 1978. From 1980 to 1982, he coached quarterbacks at Cal State Fullerton. He later became the wide receivers coach at Louisville from 1983 to 1984. In 1985, he was the wide receiver coach for the Orlando Renegades of the United States Football League. Steve started as a WR and special teams coach at USC in 1986 before moving on to California from 1987 to 1989.

He was promoted to offensive coordinator at Cal in 1990 before making his NFL coaching debut as quarterbacks coach for the Green Bay Packers from 1992 to 1995, where he mentored Brett Favre. In 1996, he returned to California for his first head coaching job.

Mariucci became the head coach of the San Francisco 49ers in 1997, and he led them to the NFC Championship Game with a 13-3 record. He managed San Francisco until the 2002 season, finishing with 57 wins and 39 losses and reaching the playoffs in four of his six seasons.

Mariucci was named head coach of the Detroit Lions in 2003 and served for three seasons until being fired with an overall record of 15-28.

Mooch has inked a five-year $25 million deal with the Lions, the highest coaching contract in the NFL at the time. After coaching, Mariucci was hired by the NFL Network to host “NFL GameDay” and serve as an analyst on the network’s four-hour pregame show, “NFL GameDay Morning.” He also contributes to “NFL GameDay Highlights” by offering follow-up reporting on Sunday’s games.
